Confounding Tools

Reflecting on COVID with open-source design tools.

Approach 1

My first approach aimed to reflect my experience of COVID as a time in which I felt an emptiness in terms of lost/missing experiences and relationships with friends and family. I wanted to manipulate old photographs of people, textures and environments to create a hazy, blurred collage.

I brought photos into the Pixlr online photo tool and used the photo filters to increase exposure and give a washed-out, undefined quality to the photos. I also gave them a rounded corner cropping reminiscent of vintage photos to again give the impression that these were old memories.

I then brought the photos in MS Word and used opacity and overlapping to create 2 different layouts. I also added some copyright-free vintage icons that were tied to directionality, time and travel.

I brought the comps into Pixlr and Photomosh to further distort the images.

Using Pixlr, I added text, then rasterized it and used the eraser tool to erase segments of the letterforms to make the text difficult to read. I wanted the text to look like there were parts missing, reflecting the concept of missing time/experiences.

Approach 2

With my second approach, I wanted to capture the feeling that COVID could sometimes feel like a societal test/challenge involving jumping between different levels and completing tasks (masking, vaccines) to defeat the virus. It sometimes felt like we were taking one step forward and then two steps back. I thought using a pixelated videogame aesthetic might convey this idea in an engaging way.

I started with research into old video game aesthetics, particularly Super Mario which so many of us played as children and included symbols like “coins” and platforms that would be familiar/readable by an audience..

I used Pixlrart.com to draw out the illustration, including various platforms, masks and hand sanitizer and other COVID-related symbols. I added “START GAME” to the bottom of the comp but later decided to change it to just “START” because I was concerned I didn’t want to seem like I was trivializing the seriousness of COVID.

Approach 3

My final approach drew on the increasing reliance on screens and virtual platforms I experienced during the COVID pandemic. I wanted to create a 3D portal connecting different screens, which I thought I could do in Blender. I had used Blender in the past for creating 3D rooms and animations, but I had never thought to use it for a traditional graphic design medium like a poster.

I created a winding pipe in Blender, applied a shiny metalic material wrap and then sliced the pipe in half via a vertical plane. I applied a cloud-like material wrap image to the vertical plane, then added further vertical planes at different intersections of the pipe/background. I applied various Photomoshed screens (Google, Discord, Slack, Discord) to these smaller planes.

I added text in Pixlr, then rasterized it and erased sections so that the pipe looked like it was winding through the letterforms. I chose the word “CONNECTIVE” to encompass the notion of connectivity through screens that was the focus of this comp. I chose the version on the bottom right as my final submission for this project.
